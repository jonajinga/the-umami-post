instructions:
  - body: "<p>Heat the oven to 425&deg;F (220&deg;C) with a rack in the upper-middle position.</p>"
  - body: "<p>Sprinkle the salt, pepper, and smoked paprika over the chicken on both sides. Press the seasoning into the skin. Let the thighs sit while the pan heats &mdash; this dry brining helps the skin crisp.</p>"
  - body: "<p>Heat the oil in the skillet over medium-high until it shimmers. Working in two batches if needed, place the thighs skin-side down. Don't move them. The skin needs 7 to 9 minutes of undisturbed contact to release on its own &mdash; if it sticks, it isn't ready. Listen for the sizzle; if it quiets, the heat is too low.</p>"
  - body: "<p>Flip the thighs. Nestle the lemon halves cut-side down and the garlic halves cut-side down into the pan. Tuck the thyme and rosemary in the spaces between.</p>"
  - body: "<p>Pour the chicken stock around (not over) the thighs. Slide the whole pan into the oven and roast for 18 to 22 minutes, until a thermometer inserted at the bone reads 175&deg;F (80&deg;C) &mdash; yes, thighs are best at 175, not 165.</p>"
  - body: "<p>Transfer the thighs to a platter and rest 5 minutes. The skin should be deep amber and the lemons should be jammy.</p>"
  - body: "<p>Place the skillet over medium heat. Squeeze the roasted lemon halves into the pan and discard. Swirl the cold butter into the juices if you're using it &mdash; this is a glossier sauce. Taste, adjust salt, and pour the pan sauce over the chicken.</p>"
notes: |
  <p><strong>Don't crowd the pan.</strong> Chicken thighs render a lot of fat. Crowded skin steams instead of crisps. Two batches over twelve minutes beats one cramped batch over twenty.</p>
  <p><strong>175 is the move.</strong> The collagen in the dark meat doesn't break down at 165 and you'll think you don't like thighs. You like thighs &mdash; you've been cooking them ten degrees too low.</p>
  <p><strong>Pair it with.</strong> A pile of buttery rice, a pot of beans cooked in the leftover pan sauce, or smashed potatoes finished in the chicken fat.</p>

The single biggest unlock for weeknight chicken thighs: leave them alone. The cooking happens to the chicken, not because of you. Place skin-side down in a hot pan, walk away for eight minutes, and the difference between sad chicken and crispy chicken is just patience.
