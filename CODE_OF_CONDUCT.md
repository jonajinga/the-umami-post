# Code of Conduct

## Who This Applies To

This code of conduct applies to everyone participating in The Freethinking Times
project: contributors to the codebase, editorial contributors (writers, columnists,
photographers, letter writers), editors, and anyone participating in GitHub issues,
pull requests, or any other space operated by the publication.

---

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in this project a respectful, harassment-free
experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability,
ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color,
religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, and intellectually
honest environment.

---

## Our Standards

**We expect:**

- Engagement on the merits of arguments and evidence, not on the identity or
  perceived status of the person making them
- Constructive criticism that is specific, actionable, and respectful
- Acknowledgment when you are wrong; willingness to update your position when
  the evidence warrants
- Respect for people who work differently than you do
- Honesty about conflicts of interest or limitations in your own knowledge

**We do not permit:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks of any kind
- Discriminatory language or conduct based on any personal characteristic
- Sharing others' private information without their explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or editorial processes
- Conduct that would be reasonably considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Editorial Standards Addendum

The Freethinking Times is a publication, not only a software project. Participation
here involves editorial work as well as technical work, and we have additional
expectations accordingly:

**Editorial disagreements are resolved on the merits.** The strength of an argument
or the quality of sourcing — not seniority, relationship, or volume — determines
editorial decisions. Editors are expected to explain their decisions with enough
specificity to be useful.

**Rejection is not personal.** Declining to publish a submission, requesting changes,
or not running a pitch is an editorial decision about fit, quality, or resources.
It is not an attack on the contributor. Editors will explain rejections; contributors
will engage with that feedback rather than escalate.

**Corrections are expected and welcomed.** Factual errors happen. Logging a correction
is not an admission of incompetence — it is an acknowledgment that accuracy matters
more than appearances. Contributors and editors should accept corrections without
defensiveness, and file them without delay.

**We distinguish disagreement from error.** "This piece makes a claim I disagree with"
is an editorial opinion — respond with a letter, a response piece, or a comment.
"This piece states a fact that is wrong" is a factual matter — use the correction
channel. Conflating these is a bad-faith move and will be treated as such.

**Do not harass article subjects, sources, or subjects of criticism.** Publishing
critical journalism does not license personal harassment of the people covered.
Coordinating or encouraging such harassment is grounds for immediate removal from the
publication.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to:
[hello@thefreethinkingtimes.com](mailto:hello@thefreethinkingtimes.com)

All reports are reviewed promptly and confidentially. The editor will respond within
72 hours.

**Enforcement ladder:**

1. **Warning** — A private written warning with an explanation of what was unacceptable
   and what is expected going forward.
2. **Temporary suspension** — A temporary ban from the project spaces (GitHub,
   editorial channels) for a defined period. No interaction with affected parties
   during this period.
3. **Permanent ban** — Permanent removal from all project spaces for severe, repeated,
   or persistent violations.

The editor may skip steps based on severity.
